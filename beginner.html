Beginner Basics

Click each section below to expand and learn everything you need to know to get started with your TI-84+CE calculator.

To turn your calculator on, press the ON key located at the bottom left corner. You’ll see the home screen appear. To turn it off, press 2nd followed by ON— the “2nd” key acts like a shift key that accesses the blue-labeled functions.

This simple power sequence is one of the first habits to master—it’s quick and something you’ll use every day. If your calculator doesn’t power on, check the battery or connect its USB cable to charge.

The 2ND key unlocks the blue-labelled secondary functions printed above each key. For example, the MODE key also has “QUIT” above it—pressing 2ND + MODE will exit a menu or screen instantly.

You’ll use 2ND constantly—clearing graphs, switching screens, or accessing tools like “TEST” or “DRAW”. Think of it as the key that unlocks hidden power in your calculator.

The ALPHA key lets you access the green-labelled functions printed above many keys—this includes letters (A–Z), special commands, and variable names. It’s the gateway to typing text and working with formulas.

Press ALPHA once to activate one letter, or press 2ND + ALPHA to lock it in “Alpha mode” so you can type multiple variables or text without pressing it repeatedly.

Graphing is one of the TI-84+CE’s most powerful features. Press Y= to open the function entry screen— you can input up to ten functions at once, each line corresponding to a graph.

Once your equations are entered, press GRAPH to display them. If nothing appears, select ZOOM → 6:ZStandard to reset the viewing window.
